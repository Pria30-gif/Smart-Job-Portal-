# Text Input Interview Mode Guide

## Overview
When your camera or microphone is not available, the interview system automatically enters **Text Input Mode**, allowing you to type your answers instead of speaking.

## What Happened
- ❌ Camera/Microphone access failed
- ✅ Interview system continues in text-input mode
- ✅ You can type your complete answers
- ✅ Answers will be analyzed and scored

## How to Use Text Input Mode

### Step 1: Start the Interview
- Click "Start Interview"
- If media fails, you'll see: **"📝 Text Input Mode Active"**
- The orange notification box explains what to do

### Step 2: Type Your Answers
1. **Read the question** in the "Question" section
2. **Click in the text box** below "✍️ Type Your Answer:"
3. **Type a detailed, complete answer** - the more detail, the better your score
4. **Watch the character count** to see how much you've typed
5. **Click "Next Question"** to move to the next question

### Step 3: Answer All Questions
- Move through all questions using "Next Question"
- Use "Previous Question" to go back and edit answers
- Try to provide substantive answers, not just one-word responses

### Step 4: End Interview
- Click **"End Interview"** when done
- Your answers will be sent for AI analysis
- You'll see your performance score and feedback

## Tips for Best Results

### ✓ DO:
- **Write complete sentences** - "Let, const, and var differ in scope..."
- **Be specific** - "Cloud computing provides on-demand access to computing resources..."
- **Include examples** - "For instance, AWS provides IaaS with EC2..."
- **Show your knowledge** - Use domain-specific terminology
- **Proofread** - Check your answers before moving to next question

### ✗ DON'T:
- **Type very short answers** - "yes", "no", "good" won't score well
- **Leave questions blank** - Empty answers score as 0
- **Rush** - Take time to write thoughtful responses
- **Use only abbreviations** - Spell out technical terms

## Scoring System

Your score is calculated based on:

| Component | Weight | Measured By |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Relevance** | 35% | How well your answer matches the question |
| **Clarity** | 35% | Quality of explanation and sentence structure |
| **Completeness** | 20% | Depth and detail of your answer |
| **Confidence** | 10% | Language confidence indicators |

### Score Breakdown
- **80-100**: Excellent - Comprehensive, well-explained answers
- **60-79**: Good - Solid understanding with good explanations
- **40-59**: Fair - Basic understanding, needs more detail
- **20-39**: Poor - Minimal effort or very short answers
- **0-19**: Very Poor - Mostly empty or single-word responses

## Example Answers

### ❌ Poor Answer
"Cloud computing is when you use computers on the internet."

**Why it scores low:** Too vague, no specifics, missing technical details

### ✅ Good Answer
"Cloud computing is a model that provides on-demand access to shared computing resources like servers, storage, and applications over the internet. It has three main service models: IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service) where you manage applications and data, PaaS (Platform as a Service) where the provider manages the platform, and SaaS (Software as a Service) which is fully managed. Benefits include scalability, cost-effectiveness, and flexibility."

**Why it scores high:** Specific, includes examples, technical terminology, comprehensive

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: Score is very low (0-30)
**Possible causes:**
- Answers are too short or empty
- Didn't complete all questions
- Answers are not specific to questions asked

**Solution:**
- Retake the interview
- Type longer, more detailed answers
- Include technical terms and examples

### Problem: Scores are inconsistent
**Possible causes:**
- Some questions have good answers, others are poor
- Varying level of detail across answers

**Solution:**
- Ensure consistent effort across all questions
- Provide similar level of detail for each answer

### Problem: Text doesn't appear to save
**Solution:**
- Make sure you click "Next Question" after typing
- The text is saved when you move to the next question
- You can go back with "Previous Question" to review/edit

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will text-input interviews be scored differently than voice interviews?**
A: No - the scoring system is the same. The analysis focuses on the content of your answers, not the input method.

**Q: Can I edit my answer after moving to the next question?**
A: Yes! Use the "Previous Question" button to go back and edit any answer.

**Q: How long should my answers be?**
A: At least 2-3 sentences per answer. Longer, more detailed answers typically score better.

**Q: What if I make a typo?**
A: Minor typos are ignored by the analysis system. Focus on content quality.

**Q: Can I copy-paste answers?**
A: You can, but answers should demonstrate actual knowledge. The system may detect overly generic responses.
